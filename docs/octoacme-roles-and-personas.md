# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own quality assurance strategy, test coverage, and acceptance validation across the project lifecycle. They work closely with developers and product managers to define and execute comprehensive test plans.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test strategy and coverage goals
- Design acceptance criteria validation approach
- Build and execute manual and automated test plans
- Identify, document, and triage defects and quality risks
- Collaborate on Definition of Done and test automation standards
- Ensure compliance with quality gates before release

### Goals
- Ensure quality standards are met and maintained
- Minimize production defects and customer-facing issues
- Build and maintain customer trust through rigorous testing and validation
- Enable fast feedback cycles through efficient test automation

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria reviews
- QA reviews and defect triage sessions
- Release checklists and pre-deployment verification
- Post-release incident follow-ups and root cause analysis

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects define system architecture, technical standards, and design patterns. They provide technical guidance, conduct design reviews, and ensure scalability, performance, and maintainability across projects.

### Responsibilities
- Define system architecture and technology choices
- Review and approve technical designs for major features
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Establish coding standards, architectural patterns, and best practices
- Guide cross-team technical integrations and dependency management
- Mentor developers on architecture and design principles

### Goals
- Ensure technical excellence and architectural consistency
- Reduce technical debt and prevent scalability issues
- Enable sustainable, maintainable systems that support long-term product success
- Foster a culture of technical rigor and continuous learning

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review meetings and design docs
- Technical risk assessments and dependency planning
- Code review comments on architectural implications
- Technical training and mentoring sessions

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches facilitate agile ceremonies, remove process blockers, and coach teams on continuous improvement and agile best practices. They champion a culture of psychological safety and iterative improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help resolve impediments, blockers, and process issues
- Coach team members on agile principles, practices, and mindset
- Maintain and improve sprint board, metrics, and visibility
- Champion process improvements and action items from retrospectives
- Support team collaboration and psychological safety

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity, flow, and sustainable pace
- Foster psychological safety, collaboration, and trust
- Ensure agile ceremonies add clear value to the team
- Drive continuous improvement through data and feedback

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-one coaching and mentoring
- Process improvement discussions and retrospectives
- Impediment escalation and resolution tracking

---

## Security & Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security and Compliance Officers ensure projects meet security and compliance requirements. They conduct risk assessments, review security implementations, and coordinate with security and legal teams to protect customer data and meet regulatory obligations.

### Responsibilities
- Define security and compliance requirements for projects
- Review designs and code for security vulnerabilities and weaknesses
- Coordinate security scanning, assessments, and penetration testing
- Document compliance impacts, controls, and audit trails
- Oversee incident response, triage, and post-incident learnings
- Ensure adherence to security policies and regulatory standards

### Goals
- Protect customer data and organizational assets
- Meet regulatory and compliance requirements
- Build secure products that earn customer and stakeholder trust
- Enable secure-by-design practices across all projects

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews and threat assessments
- Compliance audits and regulatory reporting
- Incident response and crisis communication
- Risk register updates and security training

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors represent business interests, provide funding and strategic direction, and remove organizational blockers. They act as executive champions, decision-makers for project scope and priorities, and communicate business value to leadership.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives, success metrics, and strategic alignment
- Approve project scope, timeline, resource allocation, and budget
- Remove organizational, political, and external blockers
- Communicate project status, value, and impact to broader leadership
- Participate in key decision gates, milestone reviews, and executive steering
- Ensure cross-functional alignment and stakeholder buy-in

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Maintain executive alignment and strategic focus
- Achieve business objectives and competitive advantage
- Build organizational capability and sustainable delivery

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and executive briefings
- Decision gate reviews and go/no-go decisions
- Strategic alignment sessions and roadmap reviews
- Escalation and unblocking for critical business issues

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Reference specific personas when discussing responsibilities, accountability, and collaboration patterns.
- Use persona perspectives to evaluate project processes and identify gaps in role coverage.
